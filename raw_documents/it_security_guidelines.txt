IT Security Guidelines

All employees must use multi-factor authentication (MFA) when accessing company systems
remotely. Passwords must be at least 12 characters long, rotated every 90 days, and must
not be reused across the last 5 password changes.

Company laptops are encrypted by default using full-disk encryption. Employees must not
disable encryption or install unauthorized software outside the approved IT catalog.

Sensitive customer data must never be stored on local drives or personal cloud storage.
All data classified as "Confidential" or higher must be stored only in approved company
systems, such as the internal document management platform or the secure data warehouse.

Phishing attempts should be reported immediately to security@company.com. Employees who
click a suspicious link should disconnect from the network and notify IT within 15 minutes.

VPN access is mandatory when connecting to internal systems from outside the office
network. Split-tunneling is disabled by default for all VPN profiles.

Security awareness training is mandatory annually for all employees, and completion is
tracked by HR as part of compliance reporting.
